# DentalDocs — pitch summary

**What:** Compliance-document SaaS for UK dental practices: CQC-inspection-ready policy packs, staff-training logs, and audit checklists, kept current as regulations change. £49/month per practice.

**Why me:** I spent 10 years selling equipment and software into UK dental practices and personally know 300+ practice managers. Compliance is their most-hated recurring task; most pay a consultant £1,200/year for a worse, static version of this.

**Market:** There are roughly 12,000 dental practices in the UK. We're targeting 200 paying practices within 24 months — about 1.7% of the market — which at £49/month is £117k ARR. Enough to prove the model before expanding to adjacent verticals (GP surgeries, physio clinics) that share the CQC regime.

**Unit economics:** Acquisition is trade shows and my existing relationships; blended CAC modelled at £300 per practice, which two months of a £1,200 consultant-replacement budget justifies easily on the customer's side. Pilot churn is 2%/month, giving an expected customer lifetime around 50 months and LTV near £2,450 — roughly 8x CAC. Payback in just over 6 months.

**Product scope (deliberately narrow):** We are not a practice-management system and don't touch patient data — that avoids both the integration burden and most of the data-protection surface. Documents and checklists only.

**Competition:** Agilio iComply is the incumbent; it's priced 2-3x higher and sold as an enterprise contract. We win the long tail of 1-3 chair independent practices on price and simplicity.

**Ask:** £80k angel round for 12 months: one part-time compliance writer (ex-CQC inspector, already identified) and my full-time focus.
