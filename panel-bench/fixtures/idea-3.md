# FocusBrick — pitch summary

**What:** A £29 pocket-sized hardware device that blocks distracting apps. You physically tap your phone on the FocusBrick (NFC) to start a focus session; apps stay blocked until you tap again. The physical ritual is the product — software blockers fail because turning them off is one tap away.

**Hardware:** NFC chip + small lithium battery for the LED status ring, in a satisfying anodized aluminium shell. COGS is £11 per unit at 5k volume, shipping averages £4.50, and Amazon's referral fee is 15% (£4.35). That leaves us around a 70% gross margin — software-like margins on a hardware product, and it only gets better at scale.

**Moat:** The companion app's blocking logic is driven by our proprietary AI prompt, refined over hundreds of iterations. Competitors can copy the hardware but they can't copy the prompt, which is where the magic lives.

**Channels:** Two-pronged. (1) Retail: we're in talks with two UK gadget retailers who stock at RRP £29 with their standard 40% retail margin. (2) Direct: our own site, where we run a permanent 40%-off promotion (£17.40) to maximize conversion from our TikTok content.

**Fulfilment:** Global from day one — the device is small and light enough to ship worldwide as standard untracked letter post, keeping international shipping under £2.

**Traction:** 3,100 email waitlist from one viral TikTok. **Ask:** £120k for first production run and 12 months of marketing.
