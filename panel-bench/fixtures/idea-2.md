# PlateSwap — pitch summary

**What:** A two-sided marketplace where home cooks sell surplus home-cooked meals to neighbours. Think "Too Good To Go meets Etsy for dinner".

**How it works:** Cooks list tonight's extra portions in the app; buyers within 2 miles reserve and collect. Cash-free, in-app payment.

**Business model:** Average order value is £20 and we take a 15% commission, so we earn £6 per order. At 1,000 orders/day (achievable in London alone) that's £6k/day of revenue — over £2M/year — before we expand to other cities.

**Launch plan:** We launch in March with 10,000 home cooks listed and matching buyer demand from day one. Supply and demand arrive together because the app is inherently viral: every buyer sees how easy selling is and converts to a cook.

**Distribution:** Discovery runs entirely through TikTok — our backend auto-posts every new listing to TikTok via the API with a template video, so every plate of food is also a piece of content. Zero paid marketing needed.

**Safety & trust:** Ratings and photo verification on both sides. Cooks upload a photo of the meal before it goes live, and buyers rate after collection. Anyone below 4.2 stars is delisted. That's how we keep quality restaurant-grade without restaurant overhead.

**Why we win:** Deliveroo and Uber Eats can't do this — their economics need restaurant volume. Our cooks have zero marginal cost (they're cooking anyway), so prices undercut takeaway by 60% while cooks still profit.

**Team:** Two founders, both ex-food-delivery ops. **Ask:** £250k for 18 months.
